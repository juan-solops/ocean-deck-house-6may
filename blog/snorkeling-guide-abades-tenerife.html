The Ultimate Snorkeling Guide to Abades, Tenerife

Best spots, marine life, gear tips and when to go

Abades is one of Tenerife's best-kept secrets for snorkeling. The sheltered bay, crystal-clear Atlantic water, and volcanic rock formations create a natural underwater world that rivals anything you'll find at the busier resort beaches. And the best part? It's right on your doorstep from Ocean Deck House.

Why Abades is Perfect for Snorkeling

The bay at Abades is naturally protected from the trade winds that batter the rest of the south coast. This means the water is almost always calm, flat and clear — even when it's choppy elsewhere. Visibility regularly exceeds 15 metres.

The volcanic rock shelves that line the bay create natural reefs, crevices and overhangs that are home to an extraordinary variety of marine life. Unlike sandy resort beaches, the rocky bottom here means fish, eels and octopus are everywhere.

🐠 What You'll See

  • Parrotfish — the colourful stars of Abades bay, often seen grazing on algae
  • Wrasse — curious and friendly, often swimming right up to you
  • Moray eels — hiding in rock crevices, harmless if not provoked
  • Octopus — masters of camouflage, look carefully in the rocks
  • Sea urchins — abundant on the rocky bottom (watch where you step)
  • Damselfish, bream, and salema — schooling in large groups
  • Starfish and sea cucumbers — on the sandy patches between rocks
  • Turtles — occasional sightings, especially in summer

📍 Best Snorkeling Spots in Abades

1. The Main Bay (Playa de Abades)

The main beach is the easiest entry point. Enter from the sandy area and swim left (south) along the rocky shelf. The rocks start almost immediately and the marine life begins within metres of the shore. Best for beginners and families.

2. The Northern Rocky Point

Swim north from the beach towards the rocky headland. The water gets slightly deeper here (3–6 metres) and the rock formations are more dramatic. Best for experienced snorkelers looking for eels and larger fish.

3. The Southern Cove

A small, secluded cove south of the main beach accessible by a short walk over the rocks. Often completely deserted. The shallow water (1–3 metres) over a mixed sand and rock bottom is perfect for spotting octopus and flatfish.

🎽 What Gear to Bring

  • Mask and snorkel — bring your own for hygiene; rental available in El Médano (20 min drive)
  • Fins — recommended for the rocky areas; help you move without touching the bottom
  • Wetsuit or rash guard — water temperature ranges from 19°C (Feb) to 24°C (Sep); a 3mm shorty is comfortable year-round
  • Water shoes — essential for entering and exiting over the volcanic rock
  • Underwater camera — you'll want to capture what you see
  • Sun cream (reef-safe) — please use reef-safe sunscreen to protect the marine ecosystem

📅 Best Time to Snorkel in Abades

Abades is snorkelable year-round — one of its biggest advantages over other destinations. Here's what to expect by season:

  • Spring (Mar–May): Excellent visibility, water 20–22°C, quiet beach. Best overall season.
  • Summer (Jun–Aug): Warmest water (23–24°C), busiest period, still great visibility.
  • Autumn (Sep–Nov): Warm water, fewer visitors, marine life very active.
  • Winter (Dec–Feb): Cooler water (19–21°C) but exceptional clarity. Bring a wetsuit.

Best time of day: Early morning (before 10am) for calmest water and best light. Avoid afternoons when the wind can pick up.

⚠️ Safety Tips

  • Never snorkel alone — always go with a buddy
  • Check conditions before entering — if the sea looks rough, wait
  • Don't touch the marine life or coral
  • Watch out for sea urchins on the rocky bottom
  • Stay hydrated — it's easy to lose track of time in the water
  • Tell someone where you're going and when you expect to be back
