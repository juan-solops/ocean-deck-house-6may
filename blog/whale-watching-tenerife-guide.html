Whale & Dolphin Watching in South Tenerife

One of Europe's best year-round cetacean destinations

The deep waters off the south-west coast of Tenerife are home to a resident population of short-finned pilot whales — one of the few places in Europe where you can see large whales in the wild, year-round, on a short boat trip from the shore. Add bottlenose dolphins, loggerhead turtles and occasional visits from sperm whales and orcas, and you have one of the most extraordinary wildlife experiences in the Atlantic.

🐋 What You'll See

Resident Species (Year-Round)

  • Short-finned pilot whales — the stars of the show; family groups of 10–30 individuals are seen on almost every trip
  • Bottlenose dolphins — highly active and often bow-ride alongside the boat
  • Atlantic spotted dolphins — frequently seen in large, energetic groups

Occasional Visitors

  • Sperm whales — deep-water hunters, seen on lucky days in the deep channel
  • Bryde's whales — occasional visitors, especially in spring and autumn
  • Orcas (killer whales) — rare but unforgettable sightings, usually in summer
  • Loggerhead turtles — often spotted resting on the surface in calm weather
  • Common dolphins — fast-moving schools sometimes numbering in the hundreds

🚢 Choosing the Right Tour

Small Rigid Inflatable Boats (RIBs)

Fast, low to the water, and thrilling. You get much closer to the animals and the experience is more intimate. Best for: couples, adventurous travellers, photography. Not suitable for young children or those prone to seasickness.

Catamarans & Sailing Boats

Larger, more stable, and usually include food and drinks. The experience is more relaxed and social. Best for: families, groups, those who want a full half-day experience.

Research Vessels

Some companies operate in partnership with marine research organisations. These trips are smaller, more educational, and your presence directly contributes to cetacean research. Best for: wildlife enthusiasts and eco-conscious travellers.

📅 Best Time of Year

The beauty of South Tenerife is that whale watching is genuinely excellent year-round. The resident pilot whales don't migrate — they live here permanently. That said, some seasons offer extra highlights:

  • Spring (Mar–May): Calves are often born in spring — seeing a mother and newborn pilot whale is unforgettable
  • Summer (Jun–Aug): Calmest seas, best visibility, highest chance of spotting dolphins bow-riding
  • Autumn (Sep–Oct): Warm water, active marine life, fewer tourists on the boats
  • Winter (Dec–Feb): Pilot whales still resident; occasional sperm whale sightings increase

💡 Tips for the Best Experience

  • Book a morning trip — seas are calmest before noon
  • Choose a responsible operator — look for companies that follow the Tenerife Whale Watching Code of Conduct
  • Bring a polarised lens for your camera — it cuts through the surface glare dramatically
  • Wear layers — it can feel cool on the water even on warm days
  • Take seasickness tablets if you're susceptible — take them 30 minutes before boarding
  • Bring binoculars — useful for spotting blows in the distance

🌊 Responsible Whale Watching

Tenerife takes cetacean protection seriously. The waters off the south-west coast are a Special Area of Conservation for pilot whales. When booking, choose operators who:

  • Maintain a minimum approach distance of 60 metres from whales
  • Never chase or intercept animals
  • Limit time spent with any group to 30 minutes
  • Operate with engines at low speed near animals
  • Are certified by SECAC (Sociedad para el Estudio de los Cetáceos en el Archipiélago Canario)
