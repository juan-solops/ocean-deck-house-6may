History

Sanatorio de Abona

History, mystery and visitor guide — Tenerife's most haunting landmark

Perched dramatically on the volcanic cliffs above Abades, the Sanatorio de Abona is one of the most striking and mysterious landmarks in Tenerife. Its crumbling white walls, empty corridors and panoramic sea views make it an unforgettable sight — and its history is as fascinating as its appearance.

What is the Sanatorio de Abona?

The Sanatorio de Abona was built in the 1930s as a tuberculosis hospital. At the time, tuberculosis (TB) was one of the deadliest diseases in Europe, and doctors believed that clean sea air, sunshine and a warm climate were among the best treatments available.

The location above Abades was chosen deliberately — the elevated position above the sea ensured constant fresh Atlantic breezes, while the south-facing aspect guaranteed maximum sunshine throughout the year. The Canary Islands, and Tenerife in particular, had become a destination for European TB patients seeking recovery in the early 20th century.

The Architecture

The building is a striking example of rationalist architecture from the 1930s — clean lines, large windows designed to maximise light and ventilation, and long open terraces where patients would rest in the sun and sea air. Even in its ruined state, the scale and ambition of the original structure is impressive.

The complex included patient wards, treatment rooms, staff quarters, and outdoor terraces with unobstructed views across the Atlantic. On clear days, the views from the sanatorio stretch all the way to the neighbouring island of Gran Canaria.

What Happened to It?

The sanatorio operated for several decades but was eventually abandoned as tuberculosis became treatable with antibiotics in the 1950s and 60s. The need for dedicated TB sanatoria disappeared almost overnight, and the building was left to the elements.

Over the following decades, the structure fell into disrepair. The roof collapsed in sections, vegetation took hold, and the building became a local curiosity — attracting urban explorers, photographers and history enthusiasts from across Tenerife and beyond.

📍 Can You Visit?

The Sanatorio de Abona is located on the cliffs approximately 1 km from Abades village — about a 5-minute drive or a 15-minute walk along the coastal path. It is visible from the road and from the village below.

Important: The building is structurally unstable and entry is not officially permitted. The site is on private land. Most visitors view and photograph it from the road or the surrounding area. Always respect local signage and do not enter if access is restricted.

🌅 Best Time to Visit

  • Golden hour (sunrise or sunset) — the low light is spectacular on the white walls
  • Clear days — for the best views of the Atlantic and Gran Canaria in the distance
  • Weekday mornings — fewest visitors, most atmospheric

Nearby Attractions

  • 🌋 Teide views — on clear days, Teide is visible from the clifftop
