Arico Village & Local Food

An authentic slice of Canarian life, 10 minutes from Abades

While the coastal village of Abades is your peaceful base by the sea, the inland village of Arico Nuevo — just 10 minutes drive up into the hills — offers something equally valuable: a genuine taste of authentic Canarian life. No tourist menus, no souvenir shops — just local restaurants, traditional architecture, and the kind of unhurried pace that reminds you why you came to Tenerife in the first place.

🏘️ About Arico

Arico is a municipality in the south-east of Tenerife, made up of several small villages spread across the volcanic hillside. Arico Nuevo is the main village — a cluster of traditional Canarian houses with white walls, wooden balconies, and a central plaza shaded by old trees.

The area has been inhabited since before the Spanish conquest, and the Guanche people — the original inhabitants of Tenerife — left cave dwellings and rock art in the surrounding hills that can still be visited today.

Today, Arico is known for its local wine production, its traditional festivals, and its authentic restaurants that serve the kind of food Canarian families have been eating for generations.

🍽️ What to Eat — Canarian Food Essentials

Papas Arrugadas con Mojo

The definitive Canarian dish. Small, wrinkled potatoes boiled in heavily salted water until the skin crystallises, served with mojo rojo (red pepper and garlic sauce) or mojo verde (coriander and garlic sauce). Simple, addictive, and found on every menu in Arico.

Gofio

A toasted grain flour that has been a staple of the Canarian diet for centuries. Used in bread, porridge, soups and desserts. Look for gofio escaldado — a thick, flavourful soup made with fish stock and gofio — on local menus.

Fresh Fish

Tenerife's fishing tradition is strong, and the restaurants in Arico serve fresh local fish — vieja (parrotfish), sama (red snapper), cherne (wreckfish) — grilled simply with mojo and papas arrugadas. Always ask what came in that morning.

Ropa Vieja Canaria

Not to be confused with the Cuban version — the Canarian ropa vieja is a hearty chickpea and meat stew, slow-cooked and deeply flavourful. A staple of traditional Sunday lunches.

Bienmesabe

A rich, sweet almond cream dessert that translates literally as "tastes good to me." Served with ice cream or as a sauce over cake. A must-try for anyone with a sweet tooth.

Local Wine

The Arico area produces wine under the DO Abona designation — one of the highest-altitude wine regions in Europe, with vineyards climbing up the slopes of Teide. The local white wines made from listán blanco grapes are fresh, mineral and excellent with fish.

📍 What to See in Arico

  • Iglesia de San Pedro Apóstol — the village church, dating from the 17th century, with a beautiful carved wooden ceiling
  • The old town centre — wander the narrow streets and admire the traditional Canarian architecture
  • Cueva del Viento area — ancient Guanche cave dwellings in the hills above the village
  • Local market (mercadillo) — check local listings for dates; sells fresh produce, cheese, wine and crafts
  • Mirador de Arico — a viewpoint above the village with panoramic views of the south coast and the Atlantic

🗓️ Local Festivals

  • Fiesta de San Pedro (June 29): The main village festival with music, food stalls, and traditional Canarian wrestling (lucha canaria)
  • Romería de Arico (August): A traditional pilgrimage festival with ox carts, folk costumes and local food
  • Carnival (February): Colourful street celebrations across the municipality

🚗 Getting There from Ocean Deck House

Arico Nuevo is 8 km and approximately 10 minutes from Ocean Deck House in Abades, via the TF-64 road heading inland. The drive itself is scenic — climbing through volcanic terrain with views back down to the coast and the sea.

There is also a small supermarket in Arico Nuevo — the closest one to Abades — making it the natural stop for stocking up on groceries for self-catering at the house.
