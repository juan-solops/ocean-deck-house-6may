Stargazing at Teide

A complete guide to Tenerife's extraordinary night skies

Tenerife is home to one of the world's premier astronomical observatories — and for good reason. Above the clouds at 2,400 metres, the skies above Teide National Park are among the darkest, clearest and most stable in the northern hemisphere. On a clear night, the Milky Way is visible to the naked eye, and the sheer number of stars is simply breathtaking.

Why Teide for Stargazing?

Three factors make Teide exceptional for astronomy:

  • Altitude: At 2,000–3,700 metres, you're above most of the atmospheric moisture and cloud cover
  • Stable atmosphere: The Canary Islands sit in a zone of exceptionally stable air, giving sharp, steady views of the sky
  • Low light pollution: Tenerife has strict lighting laws to protect the observatories — the island is one of the least light-polluted places in Europe

The Teide Observatory (Observatorio del Teide) is operated by the Instituto de Astrofísica de Canarias and is one of the most important astronomical research centres in the world.

📍 Best Stargazing Spots

1. Llano de Ucanca (2,100m)

A vast volcanic plain inside the caldera — one of the most dramatic landscapes on earth by day, and one of the best stargazing spots by night. The flat, open terrain gives 360° views of the sky with no obstructions.

2. Parador de las Cañadas del Teide

The only hotel inside the national park. Even if you're not staying here, the car park and surrounding area offer excellent dark skies. The Parador itself organises stargazing evenings.

3. Roques de García

The iconic rock formations of Roques de García make a stunning foreground for astrophotography. The area is accessible by road and has a car park.

4. The Road to the Cable Car (TF-21)

The mountain road up to the Teide cable car station passes through several excellent pull-off points with dark skies and panoramic views. Stop anywhere on the road above 1,500m.

🔭 Stargazing Tours

Several companies offer guided stargazing tours at Teide, including telescope use and expert commentary. These are highly recommended for first-timers:

  • Volcano Teide Stargazing: Official tours with professional telescopes, depart from the south resorts
  • Observatorio del Teide tours: Daytime visits to the observatory with guided explanations
  • Private astronomy tours: Available for small groups, bookable in advance

💡 Tips for the Best Experience

  • Go on a new moon night — the darker the moon, the more stars you see
  • Dress warmly — it can be 10–15°C colder at altitude than at the coast, even in summer
  • Allow 30 minutes for your eyes to adjust to the dark before looking up
  • Use a red-light torch — red light doesn't ruin your night vision like white light does
  • Download Stellarium or Sky Map — free apps that identify stars and constellations in real time
  • Book cable car tickets in advance if you want to go to the summit (3,555m) — permits are required

🚗 Getting There from Abades

Ocean Deck House is approximately 55 km and 60 minutes from the Teide National Park via the TF-1 motorway and TF-21 mountain road. The drive up through the pine forests and into the volcanic landscape is spectacular in itself.
