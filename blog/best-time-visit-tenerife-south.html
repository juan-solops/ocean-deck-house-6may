Best Time to Visit South Tenerife

A complete month-by-month guide to weather, crowds and activities

The honest answer is: there is no bad time to visit South Tenerife. With over 300 sunny days per year and average temperatures that never drop below 18°C, the island earns its nickname — the Island of Eternal Spring. But each season has its own character, and knowing what to expect will help you plan the perfect trip.

🌡️ Climate Overview

South Tenerife has one of the most stable climates in the world. The south of the island is significantly drier and sunnier than the north, thanks to the mountains that block the trade wind clouds. Abades, on the south-east coast, benefits from this sheltered position.

🌸 Spring (March–May) — Our Top Pick

Spring is arguably the best time to visit South Tenerife. The weather is warm but not hot, the island is green from winter rains, wildflowers are blooming, and the beaches are quiet. Prices are lower than summer and the sea is warming up nicely for swimming and snorkeling.

  • ✅ Best weather for hiking and exploring
  • ✅ Quiet beaches and roads
  • ✅ Great value accommodation
  • ✅ Excellent snorkeling visibility

☀️ Summer (June–August) — Hot & Busy

Summer brings the warmest temperatures and the warmest sea — perfect for beach holidays. July and August are the busiest months, with European families on school holidays. Book well in advance and expect higher prices.

  • ✅ Warmest sea temperature (23–24°C)
  • ✅ Long sunny days (10–11 hours of sun)
  • ⚠️ Busiest period — book early
  • ⚠️ Highest prices of the year

🍂 Autumn (September–November) — Hidden Gem Season

September and October are the island's best-kept secret. The summer crowds have gone, but the sea is still warm from months of sunshine. Prices drop significantly, and the island feels relaxed and local again.

  • ✅ Warmest sea of the year (September)
  • ✅ Quieter beaches and restaurants
  • ✅ Best value of the year
  • ✅ Ideal for snorkeling and diving

❄️ Winter (December–February) — The European Escape

While the rest of Europe shivers, South Tenerife enjoys mild temperatures of 18–22°C. Winter is peak season for Northern European visitors escaping the cold. The sea is cooler (18–20°C) but still swimmable with a wetsuit.

  • ✅ Escape European winter — 20°C in December
  • ✅ Best Teide views (clearest skies)
  • ✅ Excellent stargazing conditions
  • ⚠️ Sea is cooler — bring a wetsuit for snorkeling
  • ⚠️ Higher prices around Christmas and New Year
